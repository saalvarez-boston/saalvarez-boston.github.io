The American Mathematical Monthly
vol. 99, no. 7 (Aug.-Sept. 1992), 656-662

Sergio A. Alvarez

Department of Mathematics
University of Maryland
College Park, MD 20742

Abstract

The product of two Lp spaces is again an Lp space. Sums aren't usually so nice, but sums of sums are: if we let Lpq denote Lp + Lq for p <= q, then the sum of two Lpq spaces is also an Lpq space, and, fortunately, so is the product. We obtain formulas for the "indices" p, q of the resulting spaces and use them to show, for example, that the Lpq product distributes over addition.
