    What this course is about

    This course focuses on the time and memory requirements of the algorithms (procedures) that are embodied in computer programs. We will discuss both analysis of existing algorithms as well as design of new algorithms with a view toward achieving efficient computational procedures. Course topics include divide and conquer design, fundamental data structures, asymptotic notation, analysis of recurrence relations, greedy algorithms, search, and dynamic programming.

    • The catalog description is available in the Academics area of the CS department's web site.

    Prerequisites / Recommended background

    The prerequisites for CSCI 3383 are programming and data structures at the level of CSCI 1101 and CSCI 1102 (including a good grasp of recursion), and discrete mathematics at the level of CSCI 2243 Logic & Computation (including familiarity with basic proof techniques and mathematical induction). A post-calculus course in probability at the level of CSCI 2244 Randomness & Computation is a co-requisite. Additional background in mathematics and computer science would help but is not required. If you have any questions about prerequisites, please contact me.

    Grading

    The course grade will be based on homework and exams.

    • Homework (35%)

      Homework will be due on Canvas, in most weeks during the semester. Late homework submissions will receive no credit except in very unusual circumstances, at the discretion of the instructor. On the other hand, my "one free strike" policy will be in effect: each student's lowest homework grade will be dropped, no questions asked. This policy is intended to handle the inevitable unexpected circumstances that can occur occasionally, making it difficult to complete a particular assignment by the deadline. In order to be eligible to pass the course, you must complete and submit at least 2 out of every 3 homework assignments.

    • Midterm Exam and Quizzes (30%)

      One in-class midterm exam will be given. See the schedule below. No make-up exams will be given, so plan ahead. One or more quizzes may also be held, and would contribute to this part of your grade also.

    • Final Exam (35%)

      A cumulative final exam will be held according to the university's standard final exam schedule. Details appear in the Schedule section, below.
