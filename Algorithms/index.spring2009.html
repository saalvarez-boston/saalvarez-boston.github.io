    What this course is about

    This course focuses on the time and memory requirements of the algorithms (procedures) that are embodied in computer programs. We will discuss both analysis of existing algorithms as well as design of new algorithms with a view toward achieving efficient computational procedures. Course topics include divide and conquer design, fundamental data structures, asymptotic notation, analysis of recurrence relations, greedy algorithms, search, and dynamic programming.

    • The catalog description is available at http://www.bc.edu/crs/cs/course/cs38300.shtml.

    Prerequisites / Recommended background

    The prerequisites for CS383 are programming and data structures at the level of CS101 and CS102 (including a good grasp of recursion), and discrete mathematics at the level of CS 245 (including familiarity with basic proof techniques and mathematical induction). Additional background in mathematics and computer science would help but is not required. If you have any questions about prerequisites, please contact me.

    Grading

    The course grade will be based on homework and exams.

    • Homework (25%)

      Homework will be due in most weeks during the semester. HW should be submitted in hardcopy by the beginning of class on the due date. Source code for any programs should be included in the hardcopy submission, and should also be e-mailed to me and the TA before the submission deadline. Late homework submissions will receive no credit except in unusual circumstances, at the discretion of the instructor. In order to be eligible to pass the course, you must complete and submit at least 2 out of every 3 homework assignments.

    • Midterm Exams and Quizzes (45%)

      Three in-class midterm exams will be given. See the schedule below for exam dates. Note that one of the exams will be held immediately prior to Spring Vacation. No make-up exams will be given, so plan ahead. One or more quizzes may also be held, and would contribute to this part of your grade also.

    • Final Exam (30%)

      A cumulative final exam will be held on Wed. May 6th at 12:30 pm.
