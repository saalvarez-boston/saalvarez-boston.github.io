    What this course is about

    CSCI 2244 provides an introduction to fundamental concepts and tools that enable advanced work in modeling and computation involving randomness. The focus is on discrete probability. Topics include probability spaces, random variables, conditional probability, limit theorems, and Markov chains. Selected topics in linear algebra are discussed in connection with the analysis of covariance. Randomness in the sense of algorithmic succinctness (e.g., Kolmogorov complexity) is mentioned in passing, but is not discussed at length. Computational illustrations in Python are given throughout the course.

    How to do well in this course

    This is a challenging course. Students should plan to work hard, to keep up with the reading assignments, and to start problem sets early. Assigned reading will usually follow the in-lecture discussion fairly closely, but there may be a few exceptions to this rule. Therefore, it is important to make sure that you've understood all of the the material covered in the lectures in addition to that in the reading assignments. I cannot overstate the importance of conscientiously doing all of the problem sets in order to master the course material. When preparing problem set solutions, you should strive for maximum clarity. Your write-ups should reveal the key ingredients and the structure of your reasoning. Include detailed explanations. These comments apply equally to exam solutions.

    I'll be happy to discuss any questions during my regular office hours. If you can't make it to my office hours, please contact me by e-mail so that we can try to schedule an appointment at a mutually convenient time.

    No networked devices policy

    The use of portable computers (laptops, tablets) or phones is not permitted in class. Prior experience suggests that such devices can be distracting and detrimental to learning, both for the user and for others around him / her. Computational experimentation with the course material outside of class is highly encouraged.

    Grading

    The course grade will be based on problem sets, exams, and class participation.

    • Problem sets will be due on Canvas most Fridays by 5 pm starting in the first week of classes (but check each problem set statement for exceptions). Each problem set will be posted approximately one week before the due date. Late submissions will receive no credit except in very unusual circumstances (at the discretion of the instructor). On the other hand, I will drop your lowest homework grade, such as a zero from a missed assignment, no questions asked. The average score on the problem sets, together with my own assessment of your class participation (which may include an unannounced quiz or two), will contribute 20% of your course grade. You must submit at least seven problem sets that demonstrate a serious attempt toward completion in order to be eligible for a passing grade in the course.

    • Two in-class midterm exams will be given. You may use your own copy of the course notes or your own notes during exams. No sharing of materials is allowed. See the schedule below for exam dates. No make-up exams will be given. The average of the midterm exam scores will contribute 50% of your course grade.

    • A written final exam (see schedule below for date and time) will count as 30% of your course grade.

    Academic Honesty

    You are encouraged to discuss assigned homework problems (but not exams) with other people but you must individually design and write your own solutions for all assignments. Furthermore, you should explicitly acknowledge any sources of ideas used that are not your own; this includes other people, books, web pages, etc. You should not discuss exam problems with anyone except the instructor. Please also read the Boston College academic integrity policy. Behavior that is not in the spirit of the above guidelines may be reported to the appropriate class dean for review by the Academic Integrity Board. If you're in doubt about what constitutes academic dishonesty in a particular situation, talk to me.
